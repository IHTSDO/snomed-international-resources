Content Development Roadmap

SNOMED CT content development priorities and delivery timelines Q2 2026
⚠ Working draft · May 2026: Content priorities and timelines confirmed with SNOMED International Authoring Team. Subject to revision at each IE release.
SNOMED International's content development strategy is guided by three priorities:
Priority 1
Deepen active collaborations
Sustain and expand work with external partners (including LOINC, AJCC, Orphanet, WHO, HPO) to build content with authoritative domain expertise rather than in isolation.
Priority 2
Address the highest-priority member gaps
Respond to content gaps members have consistently ranked highest (drug extension model, mental health, imaging) through new projects and collaborations.
Priority 3
Strengthen existing content
Improve the reliability of content members already depend on. Areas selected for focused work are evidence-led (drawn from member implementation experience, CRG review, and editorial audit) and published in advance so national implementations can plan rather than react.

Qualitative quality workshop · 16 countries · 25+ real-world use cases · Oct 2025. Themes and use cases are read live from the Excel working data. Editing the Antwerp 2025 Workshop sheet will update this tab.

Cross-cutting themes
■1. Completeness & Currency
Missing/outdated terms in lab, mental health, microorganisms, vaccines. Members want more frequent updates and collaboration with Orphanet, LOINC, HPO, ICD-11.
■2. Consistency & Modelling Quality
Inconsistent attributes in anatomy, laterality, lab. Ambiguities in drug/substance/procedural hierarchies affect interoperability and analytics.
■3. Cultural & Linguistic Fit
Translation challenges and 'Anglo-centric' concepts cause misinterpretation (e.g. 'drug' in Norwegian). Members want more culturally neutral modelling.
■4. Implementation Usability & Stability ⚠
Members consistently stated: 'consistently wrong is easier to manage than inconsistently changing.' National projects need predictable update cycles and clear editorial guidance. This is the UK NRC concern validated by 16 countries: not one NRC, the community.
■5. Collaboration & Tooling
Requests for stronger collaboration between SI and national release centres. Desire for shared tools/templates for lab, microorganism modelling, and vaccine hierarchies.

Five systemic challenges
1.Content Completeness, Maturity & Visibility
Users cannot determine whether a domain is "ready enough" for implementation. Limited maturity visibility, slow turnaround, weak governance routes for missing content requests.
Pressure: EHDS-driven missing content acceleration; maturity dashboards; domain-group completeness views
2.Cross-Standard Interoperability
Shift from terminology-centric thinking to pathway-level interoperability architecture. Members want visible interoperability roadmaps, reference mappings, practical showcases.
Pressure: SNOMED as "integrator terminology"; interoperability roadmaps; governance for standard convergence
3.Modelling Quality, Legacy Content & Naming
Legacy content inconsistencies becoming implementation bottlenecks. FSN naming drift, oncology/neoplasm modelling, pathology searchability, extension vs international misalignment.
Pressure: Priority remediation programme for high-burden clinical domains; editorial consistency review cycle
4.Multilingual & Cultural Linguistic Quality
Mistranslation risk in clinical UI. Ambiguity in body structure hierarchies, inconsistent prescription wording, cultural bias in inherited content.
Pressure: Language-independent rules; multilingual representation testing; ambiguity review workflows
5.Implementation Usability, Vendor Adoption & Governance
Even where terminology quality is sufficient, vendors lack a clear implementation framework. No certification pathway, weak legislative mandate support, fragmented EHR patterns.
Pressure: SNOMED CT Vendor Adoption Toolkit: reference architecture, certification criteria, implementation playbooks
Six macro-patterns
●1. Governance clarity gap
Members repeatedly ask: "Where do we raise this? Who owns this? What is the route?" Major signal for workflow simplification and request routing transparency.
●2. Transparency deficit
Members want roadmaps, maturity views, visible prioritisation, and request status visibility. The roadmap dashboard directly addresses this.
●3. EHDS as external forcing function
EU policy landscape is clearly shaping priorities. Multiple groups connect missing content speed with EU interoperability success. NL, BE, DE, IE NRCs most vocal.
●4. Legacy debt affecting adoption
Old content issues now directly impair search, translation, trust, and vendor deployment. Not just a quality issue: an adoption blocker.
●5. Need for implementation artefacts
Strongest unmet need: toolkits, examples, and repeatable patterns. Vendor adoption toolkit, reference architectures, and implementation playbooks.
●6. SNOMED as integrator terminology
Strategic positioning: Members want SNOMED CT to act as the "glue" across care pathways and other coding systems: not merely another coding system.

The QA Programme
How QA Programme items get on the roadmap

The QA Programme remediates hierarchies where quality evidence (editorial review, structural consistency checks, and clinical audit) has identified issues that affect how reliably members can implement the content. Domains are chosen on that evidence, often reflecting implementation problems members have already reported in aggregate, rather than a single specific ranked request.

Commitment: Because QA changes strengthen the base terminology, they flow to every member's national edition. SI therefore publishes which domains are in scope for QA work in advance of each release so that national release centres can plan for the changes rather than react. This responds directly to the "implementation stability" concerns raised at Antwerp 2025.
